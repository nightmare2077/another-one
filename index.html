Cláudio Danadinho

(1970)

Mestre Claudio Danadinho is a highly respected capoeira master and musician who is known for his contributions to the art form. He was born in Bahia, Brazil, in 1956 and began practicing capoeira when he was just six years old. He trained under some of the most renowned masters of his time, including Mestre João Pequeno and Mestre Pastinha. In the 1970s, Mestre Claudio Danadinho became one of the founding members of the Grupo Senzala de Capoeira, which is now one of the most well-known capoeira groups in the world. He has since become a leading figure in the capoeira community, and his teachings have influenced countless students over the years. One interesting fact about Mestre Claudio Danadinho is that he is also a talented musician. He has released several albums of capoeira music, including "Senzala de Capoeira" and "Canto de Capoeira.

Gil Velho

Mestre Gil Velho, also known as Gilberto Silva, was born in Salvador, Bahia, Brazil in 1944. He began practicing capoeira at the age of 12 and became a student of Mestre Bimba, the founder of the Regional style of capoeira. After that he joined the group Senzala de Capoeira and became one of its most important members. He later became the leader of the group and helped to spread capoeira throughout Brazil and internationally.

Interesting Facts:

- Mestre Gil Velho was known for his unique style of capoeira, which combined elements of Regional and Angola styles

- In addition to his work with Grupo Senzala de Capoeira, Mestre Gil Velho also worked as a social worker and community activist in Salvador

Gato

Mestre Gato has almost 50 years of playing capoeira. It has been a well know figure in the capoeira scene and has in this time played an important role on the development of capoeira, organizing many meetings in Brazil and through out the world. Mestre Gato, (Fernando Albuquerque) began studying Capoeira in 1964, at the age of 16 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He began training with the Flores brothers at the Laranjeiras. During the late 1960′s in Brazil there were a number of competitions open to Capoeiristas, the premier of these being the “Berimbau de Ouro” and on the very first attempt, the team of Gato and Preguica won the competition and did so again the following year. On the third attempt the Capoeira Association prevented Gato and Preguica from entering, claiming that only students were allowed to participate…not masters!

Gato and Preguica protested that they were not masters, only students! The Capoeira Association responded saying that due to their record over the past two years they were being recognised as Mestres. In the early 1990′s Mestre Gato began studying in Newcastle at the University. As a student, he discovered “the English have rhythm and like to fight”; he organized various capoeira rodas on the University. It was such a success that Master Gato ended up creating Senzala Group of Great Britain, a branch of the original. Mestre Gato is one of the founder members and one of the leading teachers of Group Senzala and is also the head of Group Senzala: Great Britain. Mestre Gato travels to the UK at least twice a year to teach workshops and to take part in batizado’s (baptism ceremony) with all the Senzala groups.

Sorriso

Sorriso means smile in Portuguese. He has been given this nickname since childhood and those who know him understand why. Mestre Sorriso is a carioca, born in Rio de Janeiro. He was born on December 25, 1954, in "morro do Sossego," from where his family was forced to leave when he was five years old. After living for some time "here and there, on the streets, in family homes, under bridges..." his family joined the favela of Santa Marta. He discovered capoeira a few years later, at the age of nine (in 1963), after running away from home and thanks to the encounter with a person he considers as "father, brother, friend, and master": Mestre Rafael Flores, someone who guided him in his learning of this "art of life" at a time when he had no reference points.

Capoeira became his philosophy, allowing him to assert himself and express himself through poetry, singing, and music (Mestre Sorriso is also a musician and composer - CD "Bimbau," prod. assoc. Senzala, 2004). He came to Europe for the first time in the 80s and participated in the First European Capoeira Encounter, presenting shows, conferences, and workshops. Then, continuing his travels around the world, he finally decided in 1996 to settle in the south of France, in Montpellier, where he still lives today, developing socio-cultural work through the teaching of capoeira.

Garrincha

Mestre Garrincha, also known as Jose Luiz Oliviera, was born in Rio de Janeiro in 1953. He started practicing capoeira at the age of 12 and became a student of Mestre Peixinho, one of the founders of Grupo Senzala de Capoeira. Garrincha became a mestre himself in 1978 and has since taught capoeira all over the world, including in France, Germany, and the United States. Interestingly, Mestre Garrincha is also a talented musician and has released several albums of capoeira music. He is known for his unique style of playing the berimbau, a traditional Brazilian instrument used in capoeira. In addition to capoeira, Garrincha is also a practitioner of other martial arts, including jiu-jitsu and muay thai.

Itamar

Itamar was a highly respected capoeira master who founded the Groupo Senzala de capoeira in the 1960s. He was known for his expertise in the regional style of capoeira, which emphasizes acrobatics and fast movements. Itamar incorporated elements of this style into his teachings, while also incorporating other styles such as Angola. However, Itamar's approach to capoeira was holistic, emphasizing the importance of physical training, music, and philosophy. He believed that capoeira was not just a martial art, but a way of life. Itamar's impact on capoeira has been recognized by many, including the Brazilian government, which awarded him the title of "Master of Culture" in 2005. He was also a respected author and wrote several books on capoeira, including "Capoeira Angola: The Game of Symbols and Rituals" and "Capoeira: A Brazilian Art Form."
